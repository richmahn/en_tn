# I mention

"I tell about." Here "I" refers to Yahweh.

# Rahab and Babylon

Here "Rahab" is a poetic way of referring to Egypt. Both "Rahab" and "Babylon" represent the people. AT: "the people of Egypt and Babylon" (See: [[rc://en/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy]] and [[rc://en/ta/man/translate/translate-names]])

# to my followers

"to those who worship me"

# Philistia, and Tyre, along with Cush

The names of these nations represent the people. AT: people from Philistia and Tyre, along with people from Cush"

# This one was born there

"This one" refers to the people from the nations that the writer mentions. Although they were not physically born in Zion, those who follow God are natives of Jerusalem spiritually. (See: [[rc://en/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor]])
